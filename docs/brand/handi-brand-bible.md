## Brand Essence

Handi exists to reduce uncertainty around home and office services. The brand promise is simple: people should feel that the right pro is easier to find, easier to trust, and easier to work with.

Core idea:

- Practical help over hype
- Trust over noise
- Human support over cold marketplace behavior
- Clear action over confusing copy

## Mission And Vision

Mission:

- Offer a practical and reliable platform for home and office services, connecting people with experts in a safe and efficient way.

Vision:

- Become the leading platform in Mexico for trusted professional services with quality and safety in every experience.

## Values

- Trust: every message should lower perceived risk.
- Professionalism: copy should feel competent, not gimmicky.
- Closeness: speak like a modern, useful guide, not like a faceless corporation.
- Security: never trivialize safety, payments, or service quality.
- Teamwork: Handi is the bridge between clients and pros, not the hero replacing them.
- Human sense: the product solves real-life stress, not abstract marketing goals.

## Audiences

### 1. Clients / Users

Who they are:

- Busy people who need reliable help for home or office tasks.
- Often time-constrained and risk-aware.
- Comfortable with digital tools, but not looking for complexity.

What they need:

- Fast clarity
- Pros they can trust
- Safe hiring flow
- Confidence that the issue will get solved

What they fear:

- Being scammed
- Hiring the wrong person
- Wasting time explaining the problem
- Poor follow-through

### 2. Professionals

Who they are:

- Independent workers or small operators in trades and services.
- Skilled, practical, and often relationship-driven.
- Motivated by more jobs, reputation, and better stability.

What they need:

- Serious leads
- A platform that respects their work
- Clear expectations
- Tools that help them look professional

What they fear:

- Low-quality leads
- Hidden friction
- Feeling replaceable
- Being talked down to

### 3. Businesses / SMEs

Who they are:

- Teams that need repeatable maintenance, repair, cleaning, or operational service support.
- Order-oriented and accountability-driven.
- More interested in reliability and process than clever brand language.

What they need:

- Trusted vendors
- Predictable service
- Operational clarity
- Fewer coordination headaches

What they fear:

- Unreliable providers
- Disorganized follow-up
- Compliance or security issues
- Vendor fragmentation

## Positioning

Handi is a modern service marketplace rooted in trust, clarity, and human usefulness. It is not a discount-first marketplace and not a loud tech brand. It wins by making service hiring feel safe, practical, and well-guided.

Positioning statement:

- Handi connects clients and professionals through a practical, reliable platform that makes service requests feel clear, secure, and manageable.

## Tone Overview

Base tone:

- Clear
- Confident
- Close
- Useful
- Modern

Secondary tone traits:

- Warm but not overly familiar
- Smart but not pretentious
- Lightly witty when it helps recall
- Calm under pressure

Avoid:

- Aggressive sales language
- Overpromising
- Empty brand theater
- Fake urgency
- Excessive slang
- Forced Spanglish

## Messaging Principles

- Lead with the real user need, not the product feature.
- Reduce ambiguity in every sentence.
- Show trust signals early.
- Keep the next action obvious.
- Respect both sides of the marketplace: clients and pros.
- Make the service feel manageable, not magical.
- Use brand claims as anchors, not as filler.

## Approved Brand Claims

Primary references:

- No es magia, es Handi.
- Confia en los pros.
- Tu tranquilidad es nuestro trabajo.
- Donde el don't worry si aplica.

How to use them:

- Use them as headline anchors, sign-offs, or campaign themes.
- Keep them surrounded by concrete proof or clear benefit.
- Do not stack multiple claims in one short asset.

## Messaging Boundaries

Always do:

- Explain the value in plain language.
- Make trust tangible.
- Keep grammar clean.
- Use light Spanglish only when it feels natural and optional.

Never do:

- Promise instant outcomes without backing.
- Say "best", "guaranteed", or "100% secure" without proof.
- Sound chaotic, spammy, or overly ad-like.
- Turn trust into empty buzzwords.

## Visual Direction For Content Systems

Visual cues to preserve:

- Deep blue as confidence anchor
- Light blue and cream/beige as support tones
- Black for contrast and editorial sharpness
- Accent colors by service category when useful
- Stack Sans as reference for modern, confident typography
- Clean layouts with editorial and lightly nostalgic cues
- Category iconography as a structured system, not decoration

## Operational Rule For AI

Every AI-generated asset should answer these checks:

- Does it sound like Handi, not a generic ad generator?
- Does it lower uncertainty?
- Is the message easy to act on in one pass?
- Would this feel trustworthy to a real client or pro?
- Does it avoid hype that could damage credibility?
